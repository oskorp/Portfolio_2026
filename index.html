Zeus Learning — LMS Platform Overhaul

The Challenge

The LMS had accumulated 12 years of inconsistent patterns, zero design documentation, and no accessibility standards. Students averaged 4+ clicks to reach content. The platform needed to serve learners aged 12–70 across 6 countries, on devices from MacBooks to low-end Android phones — without breaking 200+ existing school integrations.

My Role as Senior Lead

Promoted to Senior UI Designer, I owned sprint planning, design direction, and quality reviews for 2 junior designers. I was the primary design liaison for 32 developers and 8 QA engineers, and presented directly to the VP of Product. Every major interaction pattern went through my review before implementation.

Accessibility Focus

Fixed all 340 violations using axe-DevTools and VoiceOver testing. Introduced proper heading hierarchies, skip nav links, visible focus states, and WCAG-compliant contrast ratios. The platform achieved WCAG 2.1 AA — its first accessibility certification in 12 years. Designed to support diverse learning styles and cognitive needs.

Built the company's first-ever design system — 80+ components with dark mode support, RTL-ready layouts, design tokens, and developer-facing documentation. Engineers now ship UI updates 60% faster with consistent, tested components. All patterns document accessibility requirements alongside visual specs.

Clio — AI Investment Assistant

Designing an intelligent portfolio companion that helps first-time Indian retail investors navigate complex financial decisions with confidence — without requiring financial literacy as a prerequisite.

The Problem

India has 80M+ demat accounts but only ~12% actively invest. Research revealed the barrier isn't capital — it's cognitive overload. Existing apps showed data but offered no guidance. 71% of users made investment decisions from WhatsApp forwards or YouTube influencers — not from within the app itself.

Design Solution

Clio is a conversational AI layer embedded within the portfolio view. Rather than a separate chatbot, it surfaces contextual nudges — "Your tech allocation increased 8% this week. Want me to suggest a rebalance?" This keeps advice within the action context, reducing the gap between insight and decision.

Behavioral Design Principles

Plain language first: All AI suggestions written at Grade 7 reading level. Confidence indicators: Every suggestion shows reasoning users can expand. Undo safety net: All AI-initiated actions have a 30-second cancel window — building trust through reversibility. These principles directly apply behavioral economics research on decision-making.

Key Learning

Transparent AI builds trust faster than confident AI. Users trusted recommendations more when we showed the reasoning behind them. This became a core design principle across all subsequent work — explainability is a UX feature, not just an engineering concern.

Tryfit — Virtual Fashion Try-On App

Solving fashion e-commerce's biggest pain point — the 30% return rate due to fit issues — through an AR-powered try-on experience designed at Corbin Technology Solutions.

34%

2.8×

The Problem

Indian fashion e-commerce sees return rates of 25–35%, with 60% citing "wrong fit" or "didn't look as expected." Existing size charts are static and don't account for body shape variation. Users default to buying 2 sizes and returning one — costing retailers ₹2,000–4,000 per return in logistics.

Progressive Disclosure Redesign

Users resisted entering measurements upfront. Redesigned onboarding to collect data contextually — only requesting measurements when they're relevant. This reduced onboarding drop-off by 52%. The AR view defaults to a "drape view" showing how fabric flows, not just a flat overlay — addressing the emotional anxiety of online shopping.
