EdTech LMS Enterprise UX

Redesigning an Enterprise LMS for 100K+ Learners

How I led a ground-up UX overhaul of a large-scale Learning Management System, improving learner engagement and reducing support tickets by simplifying core navigation flows.

Role: Lead Product Designer Timeline: 6 months Tools: Figma, FigJam

🎯 The Problem

The existing LMS was built over years of incremental additions — features bolted on without a unified design language. Learners were dropping off mid-course, struggling to locate content, and support teams were overwhelmed with navigation-related queries. The product looked dated and felt harder to use than it needed to be.

🔍 User Research

I conducted competitive analysis across 8 leading LMS platforms (Moodle, Canvas, TalentLMS, etc.) and ran heuristic evaluations to catalogue usability issues. I collaborated with the support team to extract the most common user pain points from ticket data — navigation confusion, content discovery, and unclear progress indicators surfaced as top themes.

  • Identified 3 critical navigation failure points causing learner drop-off
  • Mapped 15+ edge cases across different device breakpoints
  • Benchmarked against industry-leading interfaces for UI patterns

💡 Key Insights

  • Learners couldn't quickly understand "where they were" in a course
  • The dashboard provided no meaningful progress context
  • Module discovery required too many clicks — content was buried
  • Mobile experience was a degraded afterthought, not a real responsive design

✏️ Design Process

I started with an Information Architecture restructure — reducing the primary nav from 9 items to 5, grouping related functions into logical clusters. I created detailed user flows, then moved into low-fidelity wireframes for rapid iteration with the PM and stakeholders before committing to high-fidelity designs.

Every screen was designed with a "mobile-first, desktop-enhanced" mindset. I defined a new component library to ensure consistency across 12+ modules delivered by different dev teams.

♿ Accessibility Focus

WCAG 2.1 AA compliance was a hard requirement. I audited all color combinations for contrast ratios, ensured all interactive elements had proper focus states, added ARIA labels throughout, and designed alt text standards for the content authoring team to follow.

📈 Impact & Results

40% Reduction in nav-related support tickets
32+ Developers aligned on new design system
WCAG AA Full accessibility compliance achieved

🎓 Key Learnings

Redesigning a live product used by tens of thousands of learners taught me that design changes need to be incremental and validated — a full redesign shock can disorient existing users just as much as a bad design. Phased rollouts with clear communication matter as much as the design itself.

CMS SaaS Design Systems

Modernizing a CMS for 8 QA Engineers and 32 Developers

A complete overhaul of the content authoring system — building a design system from scratch that aligned cross-functional teams and eliminated inconsistency across 10+ product modules.

Role: Lead UI/UX Designer Timeline: 4 months Tools: Figma, FigJam, Notion

🎯 The Problem

The CMS had grown organically over years, resulting in inconsistent UI patterns, conflicting interaction models, and a confusing authoring experience. Content creators were slow, developers were confused by inconsistent specs, and QA had no clear baseline to test against.

🔍 Research & Discovery

I conducted heuristic evaluations across all CMS modules, documenting every inconsistency in a shared audit sheet. I also ran stakeholder interviews with content authors, developers, and QA to understand where they felt the most friction. The patterns were clear: inconsistent buttons, unpredictable form behaviors, and zero documentation.

✏️ Design System Creation

I built a component library from scratch in Figma — tokens for color, typography, spacing, and elevation. Each component came with interaction states, accessibility annotations, and developer handoff notes. The system became the shared language between design, dev, and QA.

  • 60+ reusable components documented with states and variants
  • Typography scale, color system, and spacing grid established
  • Accessibility annotations baked into every component

📈 Impact

60+ Reusable components built
10+ Modules aligned on one system
3x Faster designer-to-dev handoff

Mobile E-commerce UX Research

Designing a Mobile Shopping App from Zero to Prototype

End-to-end mobile UX for an e-commerce app — from user research and persona development through wireframes, usability testing, and a fully interactive Figma prototype.

Role: Solo UX Designer Timeline: 4 months Tools: Figma

🎯 The Brief

Design a mobile e-commerce experience from scratch for an organic products brand — focusing on seamless navigation, trust signals, and reducing purchase friction for first-time buyers.

🔍 User Research

Conducted qualitative user interviews with 8 participants across the target demographic. Synthesized findings into 3 user personas and a detailed journey map highlighting moments of trust, hesitation, and drop-off.

✏️ Design & Iteration

Started with low-fidelity sketches, moved to mid-fi wireframes, and ran two rounds of usability testing before finalizing the high-fidelity design. The checkout flow was redesigned three times based on user feedback — simplifying from 5 steps to 3.

📈 Outcomes

3→ Checkout simplified from 5 to 3 steps
2x Usability testing rounds completed
100% Accessibility standards met
